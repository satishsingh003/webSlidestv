Illustrated Indus

Let's play!

4500 years ago there was a great country

It was so vast, it was a country of many countries

There was an elephant country

A rhino country

A tiger country

A buffalo country...

These Indus animals loved to eat, food their favourite subject

See the manger in front of the animals?

Indus people loved animals so much they imagined everything as one

The water country was gharial, the rivers of Indus, its tributaries and distributaries

The reptile stood between lands, elephant and tiger, rhino and bison, and ate fish

This country of countries, the Indus Union, was a great idea. The Indus people imagined it as the mighty, magnificent zebu.

The zebu country was bigger than ancient Egypt, Mesopotamia, or China. It was the largest ancient civilisation: 5 million people. More than a thousand settlements. One million square kilometres. And trading further.

Indus icons are carefully chosen and finely detailed, especially the animal horns!

The zebu horns stand upright

Markhor horns twist

Urus horns bend forward

Goat horns bend back

Bison horns tilt down

Buffalo horns tilt sideways

The unicorn has a single horn

Some tigers have two horns

People have horns too

People also have long arms and lots of bangles

That curious animal with one horn, the unicorn.

It is not a real animal and does not eat.

The unicorn was a courier icon, a carrier of messages and cargo.

The couriers took stuff between countries – elephant and rhino, tiger and buffalo, bison to bison. Their icon is seen everywhere in Indus, on trade routes east and west and south and north. Across rivers and deserts, seas and mountains, forests and cities, native and foreign.

There were other fabulous animals. Animals with 3 heads.

And animals with parts of other animals and a human face.

Get familiar. We will soon play with all these animals.

Indus loved its children. There were so many toys, whistles, carts, and dolls, many of them made by children!

And there were games. Let's play.

Indus, the world's first super country

The original masters of stone and metal, craft and art, forests and cities, carts and boats, animals and plants, metrology and architecture, travel and trade, fashion and design, packaging and logistics, literacy and networking, governance and administration, from 4500 years ago.

Celebrate 4500 years of Indus, 100 years of its archaeology.
